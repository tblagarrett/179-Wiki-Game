The House Killer Rampage

Nora Ramirez

Section: Personal History

 Nora Ramirez was a 26-year-old college student at the University of Santa Cruz, California. She was born on December 16th, 1957 to Christina and Joseph Ramirez. The Mexican-American family moved from Texas to California in 1948, and leading Nora to pursue psychology in the university. Nora is survived by her parents and one brother.

 She had lived with Bobby for approximately two years before leaving the household in pursuit of living on the campus grounds. During those two years, she had allegedly worked at the Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk as a game attendant at the ladder game.

 Prior to her death, she had reportedly told her friends about her discomfort around Bobby, stating of the odors arising from his room. Friends told police she had allegedly moved out after finding strangled rats in a box in the shared garage next to his workstation. When questioned about it, he allegedly told her to stay out of his business.


Section: Murder and Investigation

 On October 30th, Ramirez’s body was found hanging on the ladder game on the boardwalk. Forensic analysis found that she must have died the morning prior to her shift, and signs of struggle were found surrounding the game.

 Inside her pocket, a handwritten note saying “scammer” was found in her pocket.
